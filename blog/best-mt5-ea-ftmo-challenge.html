📌 FTMO Rules Every MT5 EA Must Follow

FTMO is one of the world's largest prop trading firms, offering funded accounts up to $200,000. They allow Expert Advisors — but your EA must respect strict risk rules or you fail the challenge instantly.

📌 FTMO Rules Summary

FTMO enforces a 5% maximum daily loss and a 10% maximum overall drawdown, both calculated from the highest balance reached. Your EA needs hard-coded limits below these — ideally 4% daily and 8% overall — to give yourself a safety buffer.

FTMO EA Rules Checklist

  • Daily loss limit: Max 5% of highest daily balance. EA must halt trading automatically when breached.
  • Overall drawdown: Max 10% from highest balance. Hard-coded in EA, not just user discipline.
  • No tick scalping: Strategies exploiting data feed latency are banned.
  • No arbitrage: Latency arbitrage and hedge arbitrage are prohibited.
  • News trading: FTMO does not ban it, but volatile slippage around news can breach DD limits. Best practice: automatic blackout.
  • Weekend positions: Allowed but carry gap risk — strong EAs close Friday before market close.

💥 What Kills EAs on FTMO Challenges

Most traders fail FTMO challenges not because of bad strategy logic — they fail because their EA lacks the right risk architecture. Here are the most common failure modes:

⚠️ Martingale and grid EAs fail FTMO almost every time. A single bad streak doubles or triples position size — one news event can breach the 10% DD limit in minutes.

Top 5 EA Failure Modes on FTMO

  1. No daily halt: EA keeps trading after hitting -4.5% for the day. One more trade = challenge failed.
  2. Martingale or grid logic: Looks good in backtests but catastrophic in live volatile sessions.
  3. No news filter: FOMC and NFP can cause 50–200 pip moves in seconds. Fixed SL triggers, account takes a massive hit.
  4. Curve-fitted backtests: Optimised on 2020–2022 data, fails on current 2024–2026 market conditions.
  5. Wide stop losses: ATR-based SL without a cap means on volatile Gold or GBP pairs, a single loss can be 3–5% of account.

⚙️ How to Set Up an MT5 EA for FTMO

Setting up an EA on an FTMO challenge account correctly is as important as the EA's strategy itself. Follow this process:

  1. Open an FTMO challenge account — choose the $10,000 or $25,000 level. MT5 platform.
  2. Download MetaTrader 5 and connect with FTMO's server credentials.
  3. Copy the EA .ex5 file to your MT5 Data Folder: File → Open Data Folder → MQL5 → Experts
  4. Compile and restart MT5 — the EA appears in Navigator panel.
  5. Set RiskPercent to 0.5%–1.0% — conservative sizing for challenge phase. Do not exceed 1% per trade.
  6. Enable AutoTrading — the button in the top toolbar. Green = active.
  7. Run on H1 chart for each of the 5 pairs — open 5 chart windows, attach EA to each.
  8. Let it run — the EA handles all entries, management, and exits automatically.

❓ FAQ: MT5 EA + FTMO Challenge

Can you use an Expert Advisor on FTMO? ▼
Yes. FTMO explicitly allows automated trading with Expert Advisors on MetaTrader 5. The EA must respect FTMO's 5% daily drawdown and 10% maximum drawdown rules. Any EA that breaches these limits — automatically or through human intervention — will fail the challenge.
Does FTMO allow martingale EAs? ▼
Martingale EAs are technically not prohibited, but they are extremely high risk for FTMO challenges. A martingale system that doubles lot size after losses can hit FTMO's 10% overall drawdown limit very quickly during any volatile period. Fixed lot EAs are far safer for prop firm challenges.
What happens if my EA breaches FTMO's daily limit? ▼
FTMO terminates the challenge instantly. There is no warning — the moment your account equity drops 5% below the day's starting balance, the challenge is failed. This is why a hard-coded daily halt inside the EA itself is essential — it stops new trades before the breach happens.
How many pairs can I run TITAN on during FTMO? ▼
You can run all 5 pairs simultaneously. Set RiskPercent to 0.3% per pair (total maximum exposure of 1.5%). With TITAN's low drawdown profile across all 5 pairs, the combined risk stays well within FTMO's limits even if multiple pairs have open trades at the same time.
Do I need a VPS to run an MT5 EA on FTMO? ▼
You don't need a VPS to pass the challenge, but it is strongly recommended. If your computer turns off or loses internet connection while the EA has open trades, those positions are unmanaged — trailing stops won't move, daily halt won't activate. A $8/month VPS eliminates this risk entirely.
