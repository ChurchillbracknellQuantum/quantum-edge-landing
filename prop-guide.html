FTMO Challenge Strategy with TITAN

  • Start at 0.5% risk. Do not increase until Phase 1 is at least 50% complete.
  • Never manually trade alongside TITAN on the same prop account — FTMO flags strategy inconsistency.
  • Check the Journal tab daily. If TITAN's daily DD stop fires, that session is done — do not override.
  • Use TITAN PROP version only — not the Regular EA. PROP has the 4% hard stop and 60s min hold built in.
  • Run all 5 pairs simultaneously. Portfolio diversification is your biggest DD protection.
  • Target 10% over 30+ trading days. At 0.5% risk, expect 0.3–0.5% gain per trading day across 5 pairs.
  • Phase 2 target is only 5% — typically completes in 10–15 trading days at 0.5% risk.

FTMO Rules That Will Fail Your Challenge

  • Holding trades over the weekend — FTMO prohibits this on most account types. TITAN closes before Friday 21:00 UTC by default.
  • Scalping under 60 seconds hold time — TITAN PROP enforces this automatically.
  • Running the Regular EA instead of PROP version — no built-in DD stop. One bad session = breach.
  • Increasing risk above 1% to rush the profit target — exponentially increases breach probability.

FundedNext Challenge Strategy with TITAN

  • FundedNext pays 15% of challenge profits — every trade counts from day one. Treat it like a funded account from the start.
  • Run all 5 TITAN PROP EAs on all 5 pairs simultaneously. Same approach as FTMO.
  • Stellar 2-Step is the standard model (5% daily / 10% max, both static). Stellar 1-Step (3%/6% static) and Stellar Lite (4%/8% static) have tighter limits — verify your exact account type in your dashboard.
  • Minimum trading day requirements vary by account size. TITAN running 24/5 naturally meets any minimum.
  • On Stellar accounts both drawdown limits are static against initial balance (not trailing), measured on equity — floating losses count. Never over-leverage to recover losses.
  • Phase 1 typically completes in 20–35 trading days at 0.5% risk across 5 pairs in normal market conditions.

FundedNext Rules That Will Fail Your Challenge

  • Weekend holding — FundedNext prohibits this on most account types. TITAN closes Friday automatically.
  • High-frequency scalping under 60s hold time — TITAN PROP enforces this. Do not override.
  • Copy trading between FundedNext accounts. Each TITAN license = one MT5 account number.
  • Running an off-the-shelf EA on identical default settings as other clients — FundedNext requires EA settings to be customised/unique. Adjust your TITAN inputs (risk, buffers) to your own plan.
  • Running on non-validated pairs — unfamiliar market behaviour increases DD risk significantly.

E8 Markets Challenge Strategy with TITAN

  • Drawdown is configurable when you buy your evaluation — choose the 5% daily / 10% max configuration so the settings above apply directly.
  • E8's drawdown is intraday-trailing in type on Classic/One/Track accounts — floating profit raises the floor during the day. TITAN's breakeven-at-2R and partial-close logic keeps open risk small.

  • The 40% consistency rule means no single day may produce more than 40% of total evaluation profit. TITAN's 5-pair, 2–3 trades/week rhythm spreads profit across many days naturally.
  • MT5 is available for non-US clients. All 5 TITAN pairs (USDCHF, GBPUSD, AUDUSD, AUDCHF, CADCHF) are tradeable on E8.
  • Leverage is 1:50 on evaluation and 1:30 funded — ample for TITAN's 0.5% risk sizing.
